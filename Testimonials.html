Before every door.

11 years as CNA Connected in first shift Zero PRN calls that week
"

I have been a CNA for eleven years. Every community I worked at, I spent weeks just figuring out who the residents were — what calmed them down, what set them off, who to mention. Role 05 — Director of Nursing
Compliance, clinical risk
and what the data shows.

Directors of Nursing are accountable for clinical outcomes, regulatory compliance and care documentation. Remina touches all three — in ways that show up directly in their audit trails and survey results.

CMS survey — 0 citations NPI documentation 100%
"

CMS expects us to document that we are using non-pharmacological interventions before reaching for antipsychotics. Before Remina, that documentation was inconsistent at best. Staff would do the right thing clinically and then not record it. With Remina, every NPI is logged automatically the moment a caregiver confirms it. Our last CMS survey — zero citations in behavioural management. The surveyor reviewed six months of NPI records. They were complete, timestamped and tied to individual residents. That has never happened in my twelve years in this role.

Hospitalisation rate ↓ 19% Incident reports ↓ 27%
"

I track hospitalisations from our memory care unit very closely. A hospitalised memory care resident almost never returns to their prior level of functioning — it is a one-way door in most cases. Since Remina, our hospitalisation rate from behavioural or distress-related causes is down 19%. Incident reports across the unit are down 27%. The mechanism is simple: caregivers who know their residents earlier catch deterioration earlier. They notice when something is off because they know what normal looks like for that person.
